How Diego Simeone aka “El Cholo” transformed Atletico Madrid

-Piyush Gupta

It was a time of instability for the Rojiblancos, who had been through five managers in less than three years and seen young stars Sergio Aguero and David de Gea depart for the Premier League.

The team were struggling, 10th in La Liga after losing four of their past five games. So, faith was tentatively placed in the relatively untested boss whose only previous managerial experience in Europe was a brief spell with Italian minnows Catania. His name? Diego Simeone. From those unpromising beginnings, Simeone quickly formed a competitive team. His priority was to establish a solid defence based on teenage Chelsea loanee goalkeeper Thibaut Courtois and tough Uruguay centre-back Diego Godin, and that approach quickly bore fruit as Simeone's first six games delivered six clean sheets. Atletico fans were understandably quick to warm to the new boss, remembering him fondly as a key player in their team's 1995-96 league and cup double. As a player, Simeone was a tenacious, all-action midfielder - he famously described his style as "playing with a knife between my teeth" - and he instilled the same brutally competitive, win-at-all-costs mentality in his new team. It was not to everyone's tastes, but the results were undeniable. Six months after his appointment, Simeone celebrated a continental crown with a 3-0 Europa League final victory over Athletic Bilbao. More silverware followed a year later as Atletico overcame bitter local rivals Real Madrid in the Spanish Cup final at the Bernabeu.

Atletico's momentum was growing and the following La Liga season, 2013-14, was among the most dramatic in history. Against all expectations, operating on a budget roughly the same as Fulham's, Simeone's men sustained a title challenge against the megastars of Real and Barcelona. The final day saw them travel as leaders to second-placed Barcelona, knowing a draw would be enough for the title. If Barca won, they would be champions. The hosts took the lead through Alexis Sanchez, but Atletico battled back to secure the point they needed through Godin's towering header. For the first time since 1996, Atletico were champions. The common thread - first as player, then as manager - was Simeone. And he had only just started. In that title winning season, Atletico also reached the finals of the UCL where they had to play against their local rivals, Real Madrid C.F. They ended up losing the final with a Sergio Ramos equaliser with just a few seconds remaining on the clock. Some poor refereeing decisions against Atletico were made in that match. Cholo and all of the players were shattered but they quickly got up and fighting for the titles next season. Since then, they’ve reached the UCL final once again in 2016, losing because of awful refereeing decisions. In that season, they also lost the La Liga title on the Final matchday. 2018 brought Another Europa League Success after crashing out in the group stages of the UCL, along with a Super cup as well. In short, Diego Simeone converted a mid-table team to one of the elites of the world, despite having a very low budget.
